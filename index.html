Abstract

Recently, StreamVoice has advanced zero-shot voice conversion (VC) into the streaming scenario. Building on a recognize-synthesis pipeline, StreamVoice employs a streamable language model (LM) with context-aware enhancement to convert ASR's semantic features to acoustic features with desired speaker timbre. However, the reliance on a cascaded streaming pipeline, especially streaming ASR, introduces several drawbacks: 1) the pipeline complicates deployment and optimization; 2) the choice of streaming ASR significantly affects the design and performance of the VC system; 3) low-quality semantic information from streaming ASR hampers conversion stability for diverse inputs. To address these, we upgrade StreamVoice to StreamVoice+, an LM-based end-to-end streaming framework without streaming ASR dependency. Integrating high-performance StreamVoice as a conversion backbone, trained on a non-streaming ASR, StreamVoice+ extends the capabilities of this backbone by attaching a semantic encoder and a connector via end-to-end fine-tuning, enabling ASR-free end-to-end streaming conversion. Specifically, StreamVoice+, consisting of a semantic encoder, a connector, and a StreamVoice backbone, employs a two-stage training process: first, pre-train the backbone for conversion and the semantic encoder for semantic extraction under the guidance of non-streaming ASR; then, fine-tune the entire model with LoRA matrix to unlock the end-to-end functionality. To enhance decoupling and conversion quality, two main strategies are introduced: 1) residual compensation with a bottleneck in the connector to improve the ability of semantic delivery; and 2) a self-refinement strategy, using pseudo-parallel speech pairs generated by the backbone, to aid in speech decoupling. Experiments show StreamVoice+ achieves end-to-end streaming conversion with superior naturalness and speaker similarity to StreamVoice. Also, StreamVoice+ can be easily adapted to support both non-streaming \& streaming conversion.
